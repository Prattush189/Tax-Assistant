  <action>
Create server/data/cgst-act.txt containing the full text of the Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017 (as amended up to Finance Act 2025 where known).

The file MUST follow the exact structural format used by act-1961.txt and act-2025.txt so the Phase 7 splitActWithChaptersAndSchedules() function parses it correctly:

1. **Chapter headers:** `CHAPTER [Roman numeral]` on its own line, followed by chapter title on the next line (e.g., `CHAPTER V` then `INPUT TAX CREDIT`). All caps.

2. **Section starts:** `[number]. ` at the start of a line (e.g., `16. Eligibility and conditions for taking input tax credit.`). No leading whitespace.

3. **Schedule starts:** `SCHEDULE [Roman numeral]` on its own line (e.g., `SCHEDULE I`). Schedule title on the next line.

4. **No page headers/footers** — no repeated "CENTRAL GOODS AND SERVICES TAX ACT, 2017" lines or page numbers within the text body.

The CGST Act has 21 chapters and 174 sections. Key sections that MUST be present with substantive content (these are the most queried):
- Section 2 (definitions — long, will sub-chunk)
- Section 7 (scope of supply)
- Section 9 (levy and collection)
- Sections 16-17 (input tax credit eligibility, blocked credits)
- Section 22 (persons liable to register)
- Section 31 (tax invoice)
- Sections 37, 39, 44 (returns — GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, annual return)
- Section 49 (payment of tax)
- Sections 51-52 (TDS, TCS)
- Section 54 (refund)
- Sections 73-74 (demand and recovery)
- Section 122 (penalties for offences)

Include all 5 schedules at the end:
- SCHEDULE I: Activities to be treated as supply even if made without consideration
- SCHEDULE II: Activities or transactions to be treated as supply of goods or supply of services
- SCHEDULE III: Activities or transactions which shall be treated neither as a supply of goods nor a supply of services
- SCHEDULE IV: [omitted by Finance Act 2023]
- SCHEDULE V: [omitted by Finance Act 2023]

For Schedules IV and V, include just the header line noting they are omitted — this prevents the splitter from missing a schedule boundary.

Sub-sections should use the standard legal format: (1), (2), etc. Clauses within sub-sections: (a), (b), etc.

Important anti-patterns to AVOID:
- Do NOT add any explanatory notes, commentary, or annotations not in the original Act
- Do NOT include CGST Rules text — only the Act itself
- Do NOT add blank lines between every sub-section (one blank line between sections is enough)
- Do NOT prefix sections with "Section" — just the number (e.g., `16.` not `Section 16.`)

After creating the file, validate format:
```bash
grep -cE "^[0-9]+[A-Z]*\. " server/data/cgst-act.txt
# Must be >= 150

grep -E "^CHAPTER [IVX]+" server/data/cgst-act.txt | wc -l
# Must be 21

grep -E "^SCHEDULE [IVX]+" server/data/cgst-act.txt | wc -l
# Must be 5
```
