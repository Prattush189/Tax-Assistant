1. COST INFLATION INDEX (CII) TABLE
======================================================================
CII (Cost Inflation Index) lookup table for capital gains indexation calculation.
Source: CBDT Notification No. 70/2025, dated 01 July 2025
Base Year for CII: FY 2001-02 = CII 100

CII Formula: Indexed Cost = Original Cost x (CII of Sale Year / CII of Purchase Year)
Example: Asset bought in FY 2010-11 (CII 167), sold in FY 2025-26 (CII 376)
         Indexed Cost = Original Cost x (376 / 167) = Original Cost x 2.251

When CII indexation applies:
- Long-term capital assets held more than 24 months qualify for CII indexation
- For immovable property: held more than 24 months (36 months if acquired before certain dates)
- CII indexation reduces capital gains by accounting for inflation over the holding period

BUDGET 2024 NOTE (Effective 23 July 2024):
For immovable property acquired BEFORE 23 July 2024 and sold AFTER that date:
- Option A: Pay 20% LTCG tax WITH CII indexation benefit (using CII table below)
- Option B: Pay 12.5% LTCG tax WITHOUT CII indexation benefit (flat rate)
Choose whichever option results in lower tax. Both options are valid.
This applies ONLY to property acquired before 23-07-2024 and sold after that date.

3. DUE DATES -- GST RETURNS
======================================================================
Turnover threshold: Businesses with aggregate turnover ABOVE Rs 5 Crore must file monthly.
Businesses with aggregate turnover UP TO Rs 5 Crore may opt for QRMP (Quarterly Return Monthly Payment) scheme.

GSTR-1 (Outward Supply Statement):
Monthly filers (turnover > Rs 5 Cr): 11th of the following month
  Example: April 2025 GSTR-1 is due by 11 May 2025
QRMP/Quarterly filers (turnover <= Rs 5 Cr): 13th of the month after the quarter end
  Example: Q1 (Apr-Jun 2025) GSTR-1 is due by 13 July 2025

GSTR-3B (Monthly Summary Return):
Monthly filers (turnover > Rs 5 Cr): 20th of the following month
  Example: April 2025 GSTR-3B is due by 20 May 2025
QRMP filers (turnover <= Rs 5 Cr): 22nd or 24th of the month after quarter end (state-dependent)
  - Category I states (22nd): Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Karnataka,
    Goa, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Odisha, Puducherry and other category I states
  - Category II states (24th): Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Uttarakhand, Haryana, Rajasthan,
    UP, Bihar, Sikkim, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, Meghalaya,
    Assam, West Bengal, Jharkhand, Delhi, J&K, Ladakh, Chandigarh, Dadra & Nagar Haveli,
    Daman & Diu, Lakshadweep and Andaman & Nicobar Islands

GSTR-9 (Annual Return):
All GST-registered businesses: 31 December 2026 (for FY 2025-26)
Optional for businesses with turnover up to Rs 2 Crore.

6. ITR FORM SELECTION MATRIX
======================================================================
Which ITR form to use depends on your assessee type, income sources, and the assessment year.

AY 2026-27 (for FY 2025-26 income -- current year returns):

ITR-1 (SAHAJ) -- Resident Individuals Only
Who can file: Resident individuals (not HUF, not non-resident)
Eligibility conditions (ALL must be met):
- Total income does not exceed Rs 50 lakh
- Income from salary/pension, AND/OR up to one house property, AND/OR other sources (interest, etc.)
- LTCG under Section 112A up to Rs 1.25 lakh is NOW ALLOWED (NEW for AY 2026-27) -- only if no carried-forward losses
  Note: This is a change from AY 2025-26 where ITR-1 did NOT allow any LTCG
- Agriculture income does not exceed Rs 5,000
- Not a director in a company
- Has not held unlisted equity shares at any time during the year
- No foreign assets or foreign income
- No brought-forward losses (except where 112A LTCG <= 1.25L with no c/f losses condition)
Cannot file ITR-1 if: Business/professional income, income > Rs 50L, LTCG > Rs 1.25L, multiple house properties, NRI status, foreign income/assets, company director

ITR-2 -- Individuals and HUF
Who can file: Individuals and Hindu Undivided Families (HUF)
Eligibility: No business or professional income
Suitable for:
- Capital gains (LTCG exceeding Rs 1.25 lakh under 112A, or any STCG)
- Total income exceeding Rs 50 lakh
- More than one house property
- Non-resident individuals (NRI) and Resident But Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR)
- Foreign assets or foreign income
- Director in a company
- Unlisted equity shares held
Cannot file ITR-2 if: Has business or professional income (use ITR-3)

ITR-3 -- Individuals and HUF with Business/Professional Income
Who can file: Individuals and HUF who are partners in a firm OR carry on business/profession
Eligibility:
- Proprietors running any business or profession
- Partners in a partnership firm receiving salary, interest, or share of profit
- Includes all income types (salary, house property, capital gains, other sources)
Note: This is the most comprehensive form -- if in doubt between ITR-2 and ITR-3, ITR-3 applies when any business activity exists.

ITR-4 (SUGAM) -- Presumptive Income
Who can file: Individual, HUF, or Partnership Firm (NOT LLP) opting for presumptive taxation
Eligibility conditions (ALL must be met):
- Presumptive income under Section 44AD (business), 44ADA (professionals), or 44AE (transport)
- Total income does not exceed Rs 50 lakh
- Up to 2 house properties NOW ALLOWED (NEW for AY 2026-27)
  Note: In AY 2025-26, ITR-4 was limited to 1 house property
- No income from capital gains (LTCG/STCG)
- No foreign assets or income
Cannot file ITR-4 if: LLP (LLPs must use ITR-5), income > Rs 50L, capital gains exist, more than 2 house properties

ITR-5 -- Partnership Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs
Who must file: Partnership firms, Limited Liability Partnerships (LLP), Association of Persons (AOP), Body of Individuals (BOI), artificial juridical person
Not for: Individuals, HUFs, companies, or trusts
Note: LLPs cannot use ITR-4 (Sugam) -- they must use ITR-5

ITR-6 -- Companies
Who must file: All companies EXCEPT those claiming exemption under Section 11 (charitable/religious trusts claiming exemption)
Includes: Private limited companies, public limited companies, One Person Companies (OPC)
Filing deadline: 31 October 2026 for audit cases; 30 November 2026 for Transfer Pricing cases
Cannot file ITR-6: Companies claiming exemption u/s 11 (they use ITR-7)

ITR-7 -- Trusts, Political Parties, Research Institutions
Who must file:
- Charitable and religious trusts (filing u/s 139(4A))
- Political parties (filing u/s 139(4B))
- Scientific research associations, news agencies, universities (filing u/s 139(4C))
- Universities, colleges, research institutions (filing u/s 139(4D))

and 394 (TCS). This section provides the complete mapping and cross-reference
between the old and new TDS sections. Each old section now maps to a specific
"Table Serial Number" under the new parent section. The mapping below is a
COMPREHENSIVE renumbering, not a cosmetic change.

9. TDS RATES AND THRESHOLDS BY SECTION (FY 2025-26 onwards)
======================================================================
Source: Income Tax Act 1961 as amended by Finance Act 2025.
All rates are basic TDS rates. Surcharge and cess are not included.
Without-PAN rate is 20% (except 194-O / 194Q where it is 5%).

SALARY TDS:
- Section 192 (Salary): As per applicable slab rates; no fixed rate or threshold. Deduction based on estimated annual income and chosen regime (old/new).
- Section 192A (Premature EPF withdrawal): Rate 10%; threshold Rs 50,000.

NON-SALARY TDS (resident deductees):

- Section 193 (Interest on securities): Rate 10%; threshold Rs 10,000.

- Section 194 (Dividend): Rate 10%; threshold Rs 10,000 per year -- BUT threshold applies ONLY when the payee is an Individual or HUF. For companies/firms/others there is NO threshold and TDS is deducted on any amount. (Budget 2025 increased threshold from Rs 5,000 to Rs 10,000 w.e.f. 1 April 2025.)

- Section 194A (Interest other than on securities): Rate 10%; threshold Rs 50,000 for non-senior, Rs 1,00,000 for senior citizens. (Budget 2025 increased from Rs 40,000 / Rs 50,000 respectively, w.e.f. 1 April 2025.)

- Section 194B (Lottery, crossword, card game, gambling winnings): Rate 30%; threshold Rs 10,000 per transaction.

- Section 194BA (Online gaming winnings): Rate 30%; no threshold. Applies to net winnings at year-end or withdrawal.

- Section 194BB (Horse race winnings): Rate 30%; threshold Rs 10,000.

- Section 194C (Contractor payments): Rate 1% for Individual/HUF payee, 2% for company/firm/other. ======================================================================
10. TDS BUDGET 2025 KEY CHANGES (effective 1 April 2025, FY 2025-26)
======================================================================
The Finance Act 2025 rationalised TDS thresholds and rates across multiple
sections. These changes took effect on 1 April 2025 and apply from FY 2025-26
onwards. Returns for FY 2024-25 and earlier use the OLD values.

Rate reductions (5% -> 2%):
- 194D (Insurance commission)
- 194G (Lottery ticket commission)
- 194H (Commission or brokerage)
- 194IB (Rent by individual/HUF not under audit)
- 194M (Contract/professional by individual not under audit)
- 194LBC (Securitisation trust) -- reduced from 25% to 10%

Threshold increases:
- 194A (Interest non-securities): Rs 40,000 -> Rs 50,000 (non-senior)
- 194A (Interest non-securities, senior): Rs 50,000 -> Rs 1,00,000
- 194 (Dividend): Rs 5,000 -> Rs 10,000 (individuals only)
- 194D (Insurance commission): Rs 15,000 -> Rs 20,000
- 194G (Lottery commission): Rs 15,000 -> Rs 20,000
- 194H (Commission/brokerage): Rs 15,000 -> Rs 20,000
- 194I (Rent): Rs 2,40,000 -> Rs 6,00,000 per year (Rs 50,000 per month)
- 194J (Professional/technical fees): Rs 30,000 -> Rs 50,000
- 194K (MF units): Rs 5,000 -> Rs 10,000
- 194LA (Compensation on acquisition): Rs 2,50,000 -> Rs 5,00,000

New section introduced:
- 194T (Partner remuneration/interest): 10% rate, Rs 20,000 threshold; applies
  to salary, remuneration, commission, bonus or interest paid by a firm to a
  partner (including credit to capital account).

Section removed/restructured:
- 194F (Repurchase of MF units by UTI): Omitted from 1 April 2025.
- 206C(1H) (Sale of goods > Rs 50 lakh by seller): Omitted from 1 April 2025;
  Section 194Q (buyer deducts) continues to apply.
- 206C(1G) (LRS for education from loan): Omitted from 1 April 2025 when funded
  via an approved education loan.

Threshold rationalisation for TCS (Budget 2025):
- 206C(1G) LRS monetary threshold raised from Rs 7 lakh to Rs 10 lakh across
  all categories of remittances.

Compliance calendar remains unchanged:
- TDS deposit: 7th of following month (April for March: 30 April).
- TDS quarterly returns: 31 July (Q1), 31 October (Q2), 31 January (Q3), 31 May (Q4).
- Forms: 24Q (salary), 26Q (non-salary resident), 27Q (non-resident), 27EQ (TCS).
