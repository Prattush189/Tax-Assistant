INCOME TAX ACT 1961 vs INCOME TAX ACT 2025 — COMPREHENSIVE COMPARISON
======================================================================
Effective: IT Act 2025 applies from 1 April 2026 onwards.
Payments credited before 31 March 2026 are governed by 1961 Act.
Payments credited from 1 April 2026 are governed by 2025 Act.

======================================================================
1. TERMINOLOGY CHANGES
======================================================================
Old (1961 Act)              | New (2025 Act)
Previous Year               | Tax Year (Section 3)
Assessment Year             | Removed — only "Tax Year" used
Assessee                    | Assessee (same, Section 2(11))
Block of Assets             | Block of Assets (same, Section 2(17))

The 2025 Act eliminates the dual "Previous Year / Assessment Year" concept.
FY 2025-26 = Tax Year 2025-26 = AY 2026-27 (last AY used).

======================================================================
2. STRUCTURAL CHANGES
======================================================================
1961 Act: Originally 298 sections + 14 schedules; after 65+ years & 4000+ amendments,
         effective count grew to 800+ sections (with sub-sections, provisos, explanations).
2025 Act: 536 sections + 16 schedules across 23 chapters, consolidated and reorganized.

Key reorganization:
- All TDS provisions (192-196 series) consolidated into Sections 392-394.
- All deductions (80C-80U series) reorganized into Sections 123-154.
- Exemptions (Section 10) moved to Section 11 with Schedules II, III, IV, V, VI, VII.
- Capital gains (45-55 series) reorganized into Sections 67-90.
- Tax rates and regimes reorganized into Sections 194-214.
- Return filing (139 series) moved to Section 263.

======================================================================

======================================================================

- Section 392: TDS on salary
- Section 393: TDS on all other payments (replaces 193-194 series)

  - 393(2) Table 2: Payments to non-residents
  - 393(3) Table 3: Payments to any person (resident + non-resident)
- Section 394: TCS (Tax Collected at Source)

25. GST — ADDITIONAL KEY PROVISIONS FOR RAG COVERAGE
======================================================================
GST is governed by CGST Act 2017, SGST/UTGST Acts, and IGST Act 2017.
NOT part of the Income Tax Act. NOT changed by the IT Act 2025.

A. GST COMPOSITION SCHEME (Section 10 CGST):
- Available for suppliers with aggregate turnover ≤ 1.5 Cr (75L for special states).
- Tax rates: 1% for manufacturers/traders, 6% for restaurants, 0.5% for other services.
- Cannot collect tax from recipients. No ITC available.
- Cannot make inter-state supplies or supply through e-commerce.
- Must file GSTR-4 (annual) instead of monthly returns.
- Special scheme for services: 6% (3% CGST + 3% SGST) for turnover ≤ 50L.

B. REVERSE CHARGE MECHANISM (Section 9(3)/(4) CGST):
- Section 9(3): Notified goods/services where recipient pays GST instead of supplier.
  Covers: legal services by advocate, GTA services, import of services, etc.
- Section 9(4): Supplies from unregistered to registered person.
  Currently applicable only for specified categories (real estate sector).
- Recipient must self-invoice and pay GST. ITC available on reverse charge tax paid.

C. E-INVOICING:
- Mandatory for businesses with aggregate turnover exceeding Rs. 5 Cr
  (threshold reduced from 10 Cr effective 1 August 2023).
- E-invoice generated through IRP (Invoice Registration Portal).
- Mandatory for B2B supplies, exports, and supplies to SEZ.
- Not required for: B2C supplies, imports, nil-rated/exempt supplies.
- E-invoice JSON schema includes IRN (Invoice Reference Number) and QR code.

D. GST TDS & TCS:
- Section 51 (GST TDS): 1% CGST + 1% SGST (total 2%) by government bodies,
  local authorities, PSUs on contracts > 2.5 lakh.
- Section 52 (GST TCS): E-commerce operators collect 0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST
  (total 1%) on net value of taxable supplies made through platform.

E. KEY GST RETURN FORMS:
GSTR-1   | Outward supply details (monthly/quarterly)
GSTR-2B  | Auto-populated ITC statement (monthly, read-only)
GSTR-3B  | Summary return with tax payment (monthly)
GSTR-4   | Composition dealer annual return
GSTR-9   | Annual return (turnover > 2 Cr)
GSTR-9C  | Reconciliation statement (turnover > 5 Cr, self-certified)

F. ITC RESTRICTIONS (Section 17(5) CGST — Blocked Credits):
- Motor vehicles and conveyances (except specified)
- Food, beverages, outdoor catering, beauty treatment, health services,
  cosmetic/plastic surgery (unless used for further supply or as employer obligation)
- Membership of club, health, fitness centre
- Travel, hotel stays (unless for specified purposes)
- Works contract services for construction of immovable property
- Goods/services for personal consumption
- Goods lost, stolen, destroyed, written off, or disposed by way of gift/free samples

G. PLACE OF SUPPLY RULES:
- Goods: Location of goods at time of delivery (Section 10/11 IGST)
- Services (general): Location of recipient (Section 12/13 IGST)
- Immovable property services: Location of property
- Restaurant/catering: Place where service performed
- Telecom: Address of recipient (registered) or billing address (unregistered)

26. VIVAD SE VISHWAS 2.0 (DIRECT TAX DISPUTE RESOLUTION)
======================================================================
- Standalone scheme under Finance (No. 2) Act 2024, effective 1 Oct 2024.
- NOT part of the Income Tax Act 2025 — separate legislation.
- Covers: Pending appeals before CIT(A), ITAT, High Court, Supreme Court.
- Also covers: Pending DRP objections, revision applications u/s 264.
- Settlement amount:
  Disputed tax: 100% of disputed tax (110% if filed after 31 Dec 2024)
  Disputed penalty/interest/fee: 25% (30% if filed after 31 Dec 2024)
  Search cases: additional 10% premium
- Immunity from penalty and prosecution upon settlement.
- Not available if: prosecution already filed and convicted, PMLA proceedings,
  undisclosed foreign income/assets under Black Money Act.

- STCL can be set off against both STCG and LTCG.
- LTCL can be set off ONLY against LTCG.

- VDA (crypto) loss CANNOT be set off against any other income (Section 214).
- All losses must be reported in ITR filed within due date for carry-forward.

37. BUDGET 2026 CHANGES (FINANCE ACT 2026)
======================================================================
Key changes applicable from Tax Year 2026-27 under Finance Act 2026:

1. REVISED RETURN WINDOW: Extended from 9 months to 12 months from end of
   tax year (Section 263(5) amended).

2. UPDATED RETURN AFTER REASSESSMENT: ITR-U can now be filed even after
   reassessment proceedings have begun, with 10% additional tax premium.
   ITR-U can now also be filed to reduce losses (effective 1 March 2026).

3. CBDT CIRCULAR BINDING: Section 400(2) makes all CBDT circulars on TDS/TCS
   mandatorily binding on all deductors and tax authorities. Earlier argument
   that circulars were "merely advisory" is overturned.

4. TDS REFUND ON BELATED RETURNS: Taxpayers can now claim TDS refunds even
   on belated returns (Section 263(4) read with refund provisions).

5. NO CHANGES to tax slabs, capital gains rates, or deduction limits.
