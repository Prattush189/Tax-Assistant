INCOME TAX ACT 1961 vs INCOME TAX ACT 2025 — COMPREHENSIVE COMPARISON
======================================================================
Effective: IT Act 2025 applies from 1 April 2026 onwards.
Payments credited before 31 March 2026 are governed by 1961 Act.
Payments credited from 1 April 2026 are governed by 2025 Act.

======================================================================
1. TERMINOLOGY CHANGES
======================================================================
Old (1961 Act)              | New (2025 Act)
Previous Year               | Tax Year (Section 3)
Assessment Year             | Removed — only "Tax Year" used
Assessee                    | Assessee (same, Section 2(11))
Block of Assets             | Block of Assets (same, Section 2(17))

The 2025 Act eliminates the dual "Previous Year / Assessment Year" concept.
FY 2025-26 = Tax Year 2025-26 = AY 2026-27 (last AY used).

======================================================================
2. STRUCTURAL CHANGES
======================================================================
1961 Act: 298 sections + 14 schedules, heavily amended over 60+ years.
2025 Act: 536 sections + 16 schedules, consolidated and reorganized.

Key reorganization:
- All TDS provisions (192-196 series) consolidated into Sections 392-394.
- All deductions (80C-80U series) reorganized into Sections 123-154.
- Exemptions (Section 10) moved to Section 11 with Schedules II, III, IV, V, VI, VII.
- Capital gains (45-55 series) reorganized into Sections 67-90.
- Tax rates and regimes reorganized into Sections 194-214.
- Return filing (139 series) moved to Section 263.

======================================================================

- Section 392: TDS on salary
- Section 393: TDS on all other payments (replaces 193-194 series)

  - 393(3) Table 3: Payments to any person (resident + non-resident)
- Section 394: TCS (Tax Collected at Source)

10. GST — KEY PROVISIONS (SEPARATE ACT, NOT RENUMBERED)
======================================================================
GST is governed by CGST Act 2017, SGST/UTGST Acts, and IGST Act 2017.
These are NOT part of the Income Tax Act and were NOT changed by the 2025 Act.

GST Rate Structure (Post September 2025 Reform):
Rate  | Category          | Examples
0%    | Essentials         | Fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, bread, eggs, education, healthcare
5%    | Daily needs        | Packaged food, butter, cheese, transport, footwear (<1000)
18%   | Standard rate      | Most goods & services, IT services, restaurants, financial services
40%   | Demerit/sin goods  | Tobacco, pan masala, aerated beverages
3%    | Special            | Gold, silver, platinum
0.25% | Special            | Rough diamonds

Key GST Sections:
Section 9 CGST / Section 5 IGST: Levy and collection of tax
Section 16 CGST: Input Tax Credit (ITC) eligibility and conditions
Section 17 CGST: Apportionment of credit and blocked credits
Section 31 CGST: Tax invoice requirements
Section 37 CGST: GSTR-1 (outward supply)
Section 39 CGST: GSTR-3B (monthly return)
Section 44 CGST: Annual return (GSTR-9)
Section 49 CGST: Payment of tax, utilization of ITC
Section 51 CGST: TDS under GST (1% CGST + 1% SGST by government bodies)
Section 52 CGST: TCS by e-commerce operators
Section 54 CGST: Refund of tax
Section 73/74 CGST: Demand and recovery (non-fraud / fraud)

GST Registration Thresholds:
- Goods: 40,00,000 (20,00,000 for special category states)
- Services: 20,00,000 (10,00,000 for special category states)
- Composition scheme: Turnover <= 1.5Cr (goods) / 50L (services)

11. HRA EXEMPTION (OLD REGIME ONLY)
======================================================================
Old: Section 10(13A) read with Rule 2A
New: Section 11 read with Schedule III, Sl. No. 11 of 2025 Act

Formula (unchanged):
Exempt amount = Least of:
1. Actual HRA received
2. Rent paid minus 10% of basic salary
3. 50% of basic salary (metro: Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai)
   40% of basic salary (non-metro)

Salary = Basic + DA + Commission (% of turnover)
Rent receipts required if annual rent > 1,00,000.
Landlord PAN required if annual rent > 1,00,000.

Change from FY 2026-27: Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad will also qualify as metro (50% rate).
